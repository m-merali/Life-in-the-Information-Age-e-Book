Digital Divide

Impacts of Digital Divide

Economic

The rich countries keep getting richer by taking advantage of e-commerce and e-business, since they can afford to buy IT equipment and have access to fast broadband connection. The poor countries on the other hand cannot afford to exploit the advantages of e-commerce and e-business since they are financially unable and have limited skills.

In the developed world, development of ICT has created jobs for those who have specialized ICT skills such as communications engineer, computer programmer etc. However, in developing countries, ICT is used very little therefore there aren't many IT jobs available.

Cultural

There are some countries that restrict/limit access to Internet because they think it goes against their religious and cultural beliefs. Examples of these countries include UAE (United Arab Emirates), North Korea and China.

Education

In the developed world, almost all students have access to Internet either at school or at home. Those who don't have access to Internet in the developed world can visit local libraries . Students who have access to Internet can use it to carry out research on school/home work, watch educational videos and read insightful articles online. However, those students who don't have access to Internet will miss out the fruits of the Internet, and hence they may be forced to study using conventional methods.
