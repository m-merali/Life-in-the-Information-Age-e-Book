Digital Divide

Benefits and Drawbacks of bridging the Divide

Benefits

  1. Access to information and communication technologies will enable developing countries to increase their economic growth and empowerment
  2. Access to information will lead to increased education in developing countries. This will enable people of developing countries to carry out extensive research in science and technology and come up with new technologies which might benefit people or groups of people e.g. development of mobile software that would make it possible for farmers in rural areas to sell their agricultural products to buyers in the cities. This would save the farmers' transport cost as they would not need to travel to cities to receive payments or make deals with buyers.
  3. It would help in building a closer relationship between the developing world and developed world
  4. It would enable developing countries to share their knowledge, culture and research and findings with the rest of the world  hence building their global reputation.
