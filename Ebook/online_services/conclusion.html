Conclusion of online services

The advent of the Internet has been very beneficial to mankind. It has made everyday tasks enormously simpler. Imagine you were living in the 18th century and you wanted to send a Christmas card to your relatives who lived very far from your place(1000km away). At that time, it would have almost taken a week for the Christmas card to be delivered to your relatives(assuming everything went well along the way). In contrast to the current 21st century, due to desktop publishing and e-mail, it takes 10 minutes to prepare a Christmas card (this varies depending on how fast you type and design) and 2 minutes to send it to your relatives. Isn't that incredibly fast?

Online services have improved efficiency of our lives. We save more time shopping online than visiting stores physically and standing in long queues just to buy few items. Online learning has also increased the literacy rates globally and has also been inspirational for people. It has encouraged people to self educate themselves so that they can earn a better paying job. The Internet has also made it possible for us to interact with the government at our finger tips. Finally, the Internet has enhanced our need for entertainment by making it easy for us to view movies, listen to music, play games etc.

However, besides being beneficial, the Internet also has its dark sides. Hackers exploit the Internet to steal sensitive data from highly reputable companies. The data may be worth a great deal of fortune; which the companies cannot afford to loose.
